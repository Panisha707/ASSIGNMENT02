\section{Question 1}
Find a unit vector in the direction of the line passing through
$\vec {A}=\myvec {2\\4\\-5}$ and
$\vec {B}=\myvec {1\\2\\3}$
\section{Solution}
Given:-
$\vec {A}=\myvec {2\\4\\-5}$ 
$\vec {B}=\myvec {1\\2\\3}$
\begin{align}
    \vec{AB}=\vec{B}-\vec{A}
\end{align}
\begin{center}
    =\myvec{1\\2\\3}-\myvec{2\\4\\-5}
\end{center}
\begin{align}
     =\myvec{3\\-2\\8}
\end{align}
\\Magnitude of $\vec{AB}$\\
\begin{align}
    \left|\vec{AB}\right|=\sqrt{\left(3\right)^2 + \left(-2\right)^2 + \left(8\right)^2}
    \end{align}
\begin{align}
    =\sqrt{9+4+64}=\sqrt{77}
    \end{align}
\\The unit vector is calculated as
\begin{align}
    \frac{\vec{AB}}{\left|\vec{AB}\right|}=\frac{\myvec{3\\-2\\8}}{\left|\myvec{3\\-2\\8}\right|}
\end{align}
\begin{align}
    =\frac{1}{\sqrt{77}}\myvec{3\\-2\\8}
\end{align}
